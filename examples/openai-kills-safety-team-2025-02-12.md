openai just killed their safety team.

everyone's debating "is this good or bad?"

wrong question.

here's the question product managers should be asking:

if the biggest AI company just decided speed beats safety, what does that mean for YOUR roadmap?

because here's what just changed:

**1. your "responsible AI" checkbox is now a liability**

that governance framework you copied from google's AI principles?

it assumed the industry leaders would set the bar high.

they just lowered it. to the floor.

if you're still waiting for "best practices" to emerge, you're waiting for a train that left the station.

**2. your competitors just got permission to move faster**

every PM in your space saw the same headline.

the ones who were "waiting to see how the big players handle safety"?

they just got the green light to ship first, apologize later.

you're now competing against people with no guardrails.

**3. the liability just shifted to YOU**

when openai had a safety team, you could point upstream.

"we're using their models, they're handling the safety stuff."

that cover is gone.

when your AI feature hallucinates, recommends something dangerous, or leaks data - there's no one left to blame but your team.

**4. "move fast and break things" is back. but now the things are bigger.**

in 2012, breaking things meant a buggy news feed.

in 2025, breaking things means misinformation at scale, biased decisions, security holes.

the risk surface is 1000x larger. the safety investment just went to zero.

---

here's what i'm doing differently starting this week:

- building safety into sprint reviews, not annual audits
- documenting every AI risk tradeoff in writing (CYA is now mandatory)
- assuming no upstream provider is handling safety for me

openai just told us who they are.

believe them.

what's the first thing you're changing on your roadmap?
